Selected Projects

"Motivatr" Mobile App Design

Group project focused on designing prototypes for a mobile app intended to help users curb their bad procrastination habits. Design process encompassed the full UX cycle. Includes wireframes, hi- and low-fidelity prototypes, and results of user testing and research througout.

Interactive Prototype

"Zaky" Companion App

Individual project encompassing research and visual & interaction design prototypes. The app is designed to pair with a wearable sensor, allowing parents to monitor and track skin-to-skin contact or "Kangaroo Care" sessions, sleep–wake cycles, diaper changes, and feedings, as well as allowing parents to share these metrics with their pediatrician.

"Babybot" UX Capstone

Partnered with UT Psychology department to initiate UX-focused research into the future creation of a postpartum intervention chatbot application. Conducted research and interviews with Postpartum Support International helpline staff.

"SpeakerBus" Electrical Engineering Capstone

Prototype for a novel method of combining audio signal and speaker power using a software defined radio and frequency modulation techniques to model the behavior of an imagined home-entertainment speaker solution.

Eye Tracking Research Project

Research project utilizing Tobii eye-tracking hardware and software in order to probe the relationship between a subject's biometric markers (positional eye tracking, heart rate, galvanic skin response, and emotional facial recognition) and their declared assessment of website visual designs.

About Me

The career journey I began as an undergraduate student kick-started my love affair with problem solving. The biggest struggle I encountered while attaining a bachelor's degree in electrical and computer engineering was not the homework, the lab assignments, or even the projects. Instead, I fought tooth and nail against the engineering tools and software, which had clearly been developed without any consideration of the people who would eventually be using them. I didn't realize it then, but I was learning first hand about the importance of UX-focused design.

After discovering that electrical engineering didn't have the kinds of problems I was passionate about solving, I discovered the University of Texas iSchool where I learned how to listen to and learn from the users. Above all else, I believe that delivering an excellent product requires keeping the users in mind from the very beginning. Relying on the opinions of programmers, designers, or even the CEO leads to products they love, not products that people actually use.
